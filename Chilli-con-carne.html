Chilli Con Carne

Wondering what this is?

This is the latest, most succulent tasty version of the all so familiar traditional Chilli con Carne!

While it retains that classic taste, it also adds a special oomft that is sure to be loved by many.

Ingredients

  • 1 Large Onion
  • 1 Red Pepper
  • 2 Garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 heated tsp Hot Chilli Powder
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • 1 tsp ground Cumin
  • 500g Lean Minced Beef or Lamb
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • 400g can chopped tomato
  • 1/2 tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 410g can red kidney beans
  • Plain boiled long grain rice
  • Soured cream

Steps

  1. Prepare Vegetables; dice onion into small 5mm square
  2. Cut red pepper. Remove stalk and wash the seeds away, then chop
  3. Peel and chop 2 garlic cloves
  4. Put your pan on the hob over a medium heat. Add 1 tbsp oil and leave it for 1-2 minutes
  5. Add onion and cook
  6. Tip in garlic, red pepper, chilli powder, paprika and cumin
  7. Stir. Leave to cook for 5 mins. (stirring occassionally)
  8. Brown the beef
  9. Make sauce. Crumble beef stock cube into hot water. Then pour water into the mince mixture
  10. Add a 400g can of chopped tomatoes. Tip in ½ tsp dried marjoram, 1 tsp sugar and add a good shake of salt and pepper.
  11. Squirt in about 2 tbsp tomato purée and stir the sauce well.
  12. Simmer it gently. Bring the whole thing to the boil, give it a good stir and put a lid on the pan.
  13. Turn down the heat until it is gently bubbling and leave it for 20 minutes.
  14. Drain and rinse a 410g can of red kidney beans in a sieve and stir them into the chilli pot.
  15. ring to the boil again, and gently bubble without the lid for another 10 minutes, adding a little more water if it looks too dry.
  16. Taste a bit of the chilli and season.
  17. Now replace the lid, turn off the heat and leave your chilli to stand for 10 minutes before serving.
  18. Serve with soured cream and plain boiled long grain rice.
